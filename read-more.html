EXO, pronounced is a South Korean boy group formed by SM Entertainment on April 8, 2012. The current members include Xiumin, Suho, Baekhyun, Chen, Chanyeol, D.O., Kai, and Sehun. Upon debut, the group was divided into the Korean subunit EXO-K and the Chinese subunit EXO-M, with "K" representing Korean and "M" representing Mandarin. The name EXO is derived from the term "exoplanet," symbolizing the group as "new stars from an unknown planet." They initially operated in both South Korea and China, gradually expanding their global presence. In addition to the main group, there are subunits like EXO-CBX (three members) and EXO-SC (two members). Since their debut, EXO has gained significant popularity in Asia and received numerous music awards and fan accolades in China, Japan, and South Korea.

On March 31 and April 1, 2012, EXO held showcases in Seoul, South Korea, and Beijing, China, respectively. On April 8, they officially debuted with their first mini-album "MAMA," which surpassed 100,000 sales in just one month. On May 20, they made their first televised appearance with the 12-member performance of "MAMA" on "Inkigayo." On September 17, they won the Best Styling Award at the 5th Music Fengyun Bang Newcomer Ceremony. On November 30, at the Mnet Asian Music Awards (MAMA), EXO received the Best New Asian Artist Group award.

In June 3, 2013, EXO released their first full-length album "XOXO" with the title track "Wolf," along with Chinese and Korean versions of the MV. The album topped various sales charts, including Billboard World Albums, and their follow-up album "Growl" achieved significant success in South Korea, securing top positions on various music charts. On November 14, they won the Song of the Year award at the Melon Music Awards, marking their first major award since debut. On December 22, EXO reached 1 million in sales for their album "XOXO (Kiss & Hug)." The same day, they won the 2013 Baidu Entertainment Award for the Most Popular Group.

In 2014, EXO attended a New Year's greeting event with then-South Korean President Park Geun-hye on January 3. On January 16, they won the Album of the Year and Disk Bonsang awards at the 28th Golden Disc Awards. They released their second mini-album, "Overdose," on May 7, breaking pre-order records with nearly 660,000 copies. On November 21, they won three awards at the SBS Gayo Daejeon, including Best Male Group and Best Album.


On January 22, 2015, EXO won the Daesang, Bonsang, and iQiyi Popularity Awards at the 24th Seoul Music Awards. In March, they topped Forbes Korea's list of "40 Power Celebrities." They held their second solo concert, "EXO Planet #2 – The EXO'luXion," from March to August, starting in Seoul and expanding to other cities. On May 7, they released their second full-length album, "EXODUS," which ranked 90th on the Billboard 200. In November, EXO made their Japanese debut and held a three-day concert in Tokyo Dome.

In 2016, EXO received the Daesang, Bonsang, and Hallyu Special Awards at the 25th Seoul Music Awards. They released their third full-length album, "EX'ACT," in June, breaking the record for the highest pre-sale volume in Korean music history. They held their third solo concert in Seoul in July. In November, they received the Baidu Entertainment Annual Person Award.

In 2017, EXO held their third solo concert encore, "EXO PLANET #3 – The EXO'rDIUM[dot]," in Seoul. On July 18, they released their fourth full-length album, "THE WAR." In September, they released the repackaged album "The Power of Music." In November, they held their fourth solo concert in Seoul. On December 21, they released their fourth winter special album, "Universe."

In 2018, EXO won Album of the Year and the newly established PGA Global Popularity Award at the 32nd Golden Disc Awards. On January 31, they released their first Japanese full-length album, "COUNTDOWN," which topped the Oricon weekly chart. On November 2, they released their fifth full-length album, "Don't Mess Up My Tempo," and on December 14, they released the repackaged album "Love Shot."

In 2019, EXO topped the Korean idol chart for three consecutive weeks. On November 27, they released their sixth full-length album, "OBSESSION."

On June 7, 2021, they released the special album "Don't Fight the Feeling." In December, they won the TMEA Tencent Music Entertainment Awards for Best Overseas Group. On April 8, 2022, Lay announced the expiration of his contract with SM Entertainment on social media.

In June 19, 2023, it was reported that Baekhyun, Xiumin, Chen, and SM Entertainment reached a settlement, confirming that the contract will continue, and EXO's activities will continue. On July 10, they released their seventh full-length album, "EXIST."
